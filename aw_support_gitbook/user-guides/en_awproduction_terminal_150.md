                                           Production Terminals
                                           A+W Production Terminals are software modules of the production control
                                           system A+W Production which serve to check, enter, modify, control, and di-
                                           vert process steps in the work flow.
                                           A+W Production Terminals visualize and control individual processing steps
                                           and can be configured individually. A+W Production Terminals can be used to
                                           print labels and reports online, and enter breakage including all necessary in-
                                           formation. Operation of the production terminals is easy and uniform. These
                                           intelligent systems enable you to inform your customers of the current state of

                                        A brief explanation of the individual Terminals:

                                        Terminal Manual Cutting Visualizes the work schedule for the sheets to be
                                        cut at the manual cutting table.

                                        Terminal Order Is used wherever large quantities are to be booked.

                                        Terminal Processing Is the production terminal version used with the pro-
                                        cessing machinery, automatically controlling the machines. This module pro-
                                        vides detailed information on the processing steps to be performed including
                                        true-to-scale production drawings.

                                        Terminal IG-In Is installed at the IG line entrance and visualizes the work
                                        schedule for the sheets of a batch or of a logical production rack/harp rack, in
                                        production sequence.

                                        Terminal IG-Assembly Is installed between the visual check of sheets after
                                        the washer, and in front of the spacer applicator, visualizing the technical data
                                        including the spacer data for each unit to be produced.

                                        Terminal IG-Out Installed at the IG line exit and shows the technical and
                                        shipping-related data for each unit produced.

                                        Terminal TG-In Is the display system at the furnace entry. It shows the fur-
                                        nace bed load and is controlled by the operator. All sheets are scanned; their
                                        geometry is checked for completeness of processing.

                                        Terminal TG-Out This is the display system at the furnace exit. All sheets
                                        registered at the furnace entry are displayed based on the clock rate. If de-
                                        fined, the user will get information as to the further use or on stacking in the
                                        shipping area.

                                        Terminal Edit Is the control station version that is normally used in the ship-
                                        ping office for rebooking units, sheets, or groups and for managing off-site
                                        racks.

                                        Terminal LG-In Installed at the laminating line entrance, it visualizes the work
                                        schedule for the sheets of a batch or a logical production rack/harp rack to be
                                        produced, in production sequence.

                                        Terminal LG-Assembly Installed in the clean room, it shows the work sched-
                                        ule after a sheet or unit has been booked at Terminal LG-In. This leaves ample
                                        time for cutting the necessary film layers.

                                           Goals of the Production Terminal Tech-
                                           nique
                                           The following goals shall be achieved by means of the production terminal
                                           technique:
                                           •   Scheduled and optimised jobs or batches are distributed to the appropriate
                                               workstations.
                                           •   The workers at the machines are provided with graphical information on all
                                               relevant details of the sheets (shape, alignment, sizes, processing).
                                           •   The work performed is booked.
                                           •   Deviations from detailed scheduling are registered.
                                           •   Scheduled and actual production processes are compared during produc-
                                               tion.
                                           •   The production process is visualized.
                                           •   Remakes (due to breakage) are created automatically.
                                           •   The workers at the machines are provided with all relevant information on
                                               the sheets (shape, alignment, sizes, processing).
                                           •   The production process can be re-optimised manually.
                                           •   The collected data are used for analyses.
                                           Each of the production terminal modules provides special support for one of
                                           these goals.

                                               Performance counter
                                               Additional fields can be displayed in the A+W Production Terminals, which
                                               show the produced quantity, area and linear meters for a specific time at a
                                               registration point. Please contact an A+W employee in this regard.


                                           Operation of the Production Terminals
                                           To use the production terminal technology to the best effect, barcodes are reg-
                                           istered in all relevant places. The appropriate workstations or machines are
                                           equipped with barcode scanners. A barcode scanner is firmly allocated to a
                                           workstation (registration point). Barcodes serve to identify production factors,
                                           processes, or states such as:
                                           •   Sheets (single sheets or groups)
                                           •   Racks
                                           •   Employees
                                           •   Processing steps
                                           •   Breakage
                                           If a rack barcode is canned for example, the software will book the correspond-
                                           ing rack automatically to a defined processing station for instance.
                                           If the same scanner is used to scan a sheet barcode, the software books the
1.50 / 01-2023




                                           sheet e.g. to the currently registered rack.
                                           System configuration defines the actions to be performed automatically upon
                                           scanning the barcodes.

                                           Using Terminal IG-In
                                           Objectives

                                           •   Introducing the user interface of Terminal IG-In
                                           •   Getting to know and understanding the operation
                                           •   Correct handling of intermissions
                                           •   Being able to handle unforeseeable situations (e.g. shortage in material)


                                           Benefit

                                           Terminal IG-In supports you when it comes to loading the sheets of a unit from rack to
                                           the IG line in the correct sequence. To work efficiently you have to understand the
                                           functioning of the program; you must also be capable of handling daily recurring
                                           situations.


                                           Definitions

                                           Barcode                      Are opto-electronically readable signs consisting of
                                                                        parallel lines and gaps of different width.

                                           Batch                        Amount of items scheduled and produced together.

                                           Lot                          Amount of elements which are processed together on
                                                                        the same machine.

                                           Remake                       Sheet that has been recut due to breakage or a defect.

                                           batch                        Amount of goods with identical properties. Example: a
                                                                        barrel of butyl.

                                           Breakage                     Defective sheet that cannot be used.

                                           Production number            Defines/shows the production sequence within a batch.
                                                                        Example: production number 22 is the 22nd IG unit to be
                                                                        produced.

                                           Scanning                     The term Scanning defines the registration of one or
                                                                        more barcodes.

                                           Book                         The Booking process writes and saves certain data in
                                                                        the database.


                                           Note

                                           Intermissions                Must be recorded, always.

                                           Breakage reasons             Have to be documented always.

                                           Operating Sequence
                                           The operating sequence in Terminal IG-In is:
                                           •   Start module Terminal IG-In.
                                           •   Log in (<Name>).
                                           •   Load a lot.
                                           •   Produce the sheet.
                                           You have to record every interruption of your work at the machine. Reasons
                                           for an interruption may be:
                                           •   Machine maintenance
                                           •   Machine failure
                                           •   Breaks
                                           If a sheet you are about to book proves to be defective you have to book the
                                           cause of the defect as well. Causes of defects can be:
                                           •   Breakage
                                           •   Scratches
                                           •   Wrong sizes
                                           •   Cracks


                                            How to register
                                           1 Start Terminal IG-In.
                                           2 Go to menu File and open menu item Reregister or press control key [F4].
                                             Dialog ToolTV Registration opens.




                                           3 Enter your name in field User (e.g. Smith).
                                           4 Press [OK].
                                           5 The dialog closes and you will find yourself back in the main window.

                                               Scan your name
                                               If you are using a scanner you can scan right in the main window without
                                               the need to open the ToolTV Registration dialog.
